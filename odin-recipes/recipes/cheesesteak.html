Recipe #4 - Big Kahuna Cheese Steak

Description

The one and only choice picked at Jersey Mike’s and my favorite sandwich to eat. Rather, it's a hot sub.

Melted my tounge like the cheese fries at Studio Movie Grill. There's nothing much to really talk about.

For the size and price, it's not really worth it.

Ingredient List

  • 1 lb. beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 8 slices of provolone cheese
  • 4 sub rolls
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 tbsp olive oil

Instructions

These instructions are for the normal-sized sandwich instead of the double/super-sized variant. I'll include because there are instructions and it'd be disrespectful to not include it.

Also, it's highly unlikely anyone has the equipment to properly make this sandwich in the first place. I mean, the ingredients themselves are varied and most likely super duper ultra expensive.

  • Add the sliced onion and peppers. Cook until they are tender and lightly caramelized, about 10-12 minutes. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  • In the same skillet, add 2 tbsp of olive oil and the thinly sliced beef. Cook until the beef is browned and cooked through, about 6-8 minutes.
  • Preheat the broiler on high.
  • Slice the rolls in half and place them under the broiler until lightly toasted.
  • On the bottom half of each roll, add a few slices of beef, followed by a generous helping of the sautéed onions and peppers. Top with 2 slices of provolone cheese.
  • Place the sandwiches back under the broiler until the cheese is melted and bubbly, about 2-3 minutes.
  • Put the top half of the roll on the sandwich and serve hot.
